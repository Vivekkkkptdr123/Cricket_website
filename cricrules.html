Cricket Rules

Players and Teams

A cricket team consists of 11 players, with one designated as the captain. Each team takes turns batting and bowling/fielding.

The Toss

Before the match begins, a coin toss is conducted to determine which team will bat or bowl first.

The Pitch

The playing field, known as the pitch, is typically 22 yards long and made of grass. It has wickets at each end.

Wickets

Each wicket consists of three stumps (vertical wooden posts) topped by two bails (horizontal wooden pieces). The aim of the bowling team is to hit the stumps to dismiss the batsman.

Scoring Runs

Batsmen score runs by hitting the ball and running between the wickets. They can also score runs by hitting boundaries (4 or 6 runs) by hitting the ball to or over the boundary ropes.

Dismissals

Batsmen can be dismissed in various ways, including being bowled, caught, run out, stumped, and LBW (Leg Before Wicket).

Overs

An over consists of six legal deliveries bowled by a single bowler. The number of overs in a match depends on the format.

Fielding Restrictions

In limited-overs cricket, there are restrictions on the placement of fielders during certain phases of the game, such as powerplays.

No Balls

A no ball is an illegal delivery by the bowler, resulting in the batting side being awarded an extra run and any runs scored off that delivery.

Wide Balls

A wide ball is a delivery that is too far from the batsman for a fair attempt to hit it. It results in the batting side being awarded an extra run.

Free Hits

In limited-overs cricket, a no-ball due to overstepping by the bowler results in a "free hit" for the batsman, meaning they cannot be dismissed except through a run-out.

Umpires

There are two on-field umpires who officiate the match, making decisions on dismissals, boundaries, and other aspects of play.

Reviews

Teams have the option to request a review of the umpire's decision using the Decision Review System (DRS) a limited number of times per innings.

Spirit of Cricket

Players are expected to adhere to the "spirit of cricket," which promotes fair play, sportsmanship, and respect for opponents and officials.

Penalties

Violations of the rules can result in penalties, such as runs being awarded to the opposing team or the offending player being suspended.
